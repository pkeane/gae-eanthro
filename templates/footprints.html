Footprints Exercise

On the Track of Prehistoric Humans

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

What do the footprints at Laetoli tell us about the prehistoric hominins that left them behind? YOU can measure and assess the correlation between foot length and stride length versus body height and then use these data along with those from other students to estimate the height of the Laetoli hominins. You can then use these data to better understand the fossil record.

Step 1:

Take off your shoes, trace the outline of your foot on the reverse of this sheet, and measure the total length of your foot with the provided ruler in cm. Record your first name, age, and todayâ€™s date on the form. Circle if you are male or female, and write your foot measurement in the space marked "Foot Length (cm)".

Step 2:

Measure your height in cm on the scale and record this measurement in the space marked "Stature (cm)".

Step 3:

Step into the basin of fingerpaint (or here, toepaint!).

Step 4:

Walk carefully and normally for several steps across the butcher paper.

Step 5:

Wash and dry your feet at the end, and then use the ruler to measure the length of your stride in cm. You stride is the distance between the back of your heel on one step and the back of your heel on the second step of the same foot. For example, take three steps starting with your right foot, and then measure the distance between your first step taken with the right foot and the second step taken with the right foot. Record this measurement in the space marked "Stride Length (cm)".

Step 6:

Plot your data on the graph by entering the information on the provided form. On the graph, RED represents girls and BLUE represents boys.
